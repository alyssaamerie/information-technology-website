The Field of Information Technology

What is Information Technology?

Information Technology (IT) is a broad field encompassing the use of computer systems, software, and networks to manage, store, and transmit information. It involves various functions like building communication networks, securing data, and troubleshooting computer problems.

Scope of Information Technology-

Uses of Information Technology
A few uses of information technology include:
  1. Facilitates Communication- IT enables seamless communication and collaboration across different locations and time zones.
  2. Business Operations- IT allows smooth functioning of modern businesses, from managing operations and finances to marketing and customer service.
  3. Education- IT facilitates learning from anywhere through the use of digital classrooms and providing access to educational resources online.
Limitations of Information Technology
A few limitations of information technology include:
  1. IT systems are vulnerable to cybersecurity risks such as hacking,cyberattacks, viruses and data breaches.
  2. Implementing and maintaining IT infrastructure can be expensive.
  3. There can be issues related to data privacy and the potential for misuse of technology.

History of the Internet and Telecommunications

Telecommunications

The transmission of data, voice and video over distances using Technology
Developments-
  • 1830s- Telegraph invention enabled long-distance communication
  • 1876- Alexander Graham Bell invented the telephone
  • 20th Century- Raido, televison and satellites revoluntionized communication
  • Present- Mobile networks (4G,5G), fiber optics and VolP enhance global connectivity

Internet

A worldwide netwrok connecting computers, allowing for instant communication and data sharing
Developments-
  • 1960s- ARPANET, the first version of the internet, was developed by the U.S military
  • 1980s-1990s- The World Wide Web (WWW) was created by Tim Berners-Lee, leading to web browsers and email services
  • 2000s-Present- High-speed broadband, cloud computing, and social media redefine digital communication

Data, Information and Knowledge

Data

  • Definition- Raw, unprocessed facts and figures
  • Characteristics- Unorganized, lacks its own meaning, qualitative (opinions) or quantitave (numbers and measurements)
  • Example- A list of numbers (70,50, 55) or random words (orange, apple)

Information

  • Definition- Processed data that has meaning and context
  • Characteristics- It should be valuable, relevant, confidential, timely and secure
  • Example- The numbers (70,50,55) being labeled as student scores in an English test, become information because they now have meaning

Knowledge

  • Definiton-Using information effectively fro decision making
  • Characteristics- Involves critical thinking, analyzing data quality, and ensuring informationis appropriate for decision-making
  • Example- The teachers sees that most students scored less than 80, so they need extra lessons
